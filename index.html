How this is calculated. Plant-date yield curve is from University of Illinois trials (Nafziger et al., 2007–2024) covering >20 years across central and northern IL, calibrated against 2025 farm-level results from the central Illinois region the tool serves. Soil-temperature thresholds and imbibitional-chilling guidance follow Iowa State, UNL CropWatch, and Peterson Ag agronomy bulletins. GDU uses base 50 °F / cap 86 °F. The decision logic blends three things: where you are on the yield-potential calendar, whether the next 48 hours protect the seed, and how fast the heat will pull the crop out of the ground.
